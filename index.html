The Bugatti Veyron EB 16.4 was developed by the Volkswagen Group and Bugatti, manufactured in Molsheim, France, and named after French racing driver Pierre Veyron, who won the 1939 24 Hours of Le Mans WikipediaWikipedia+1. Introduced in 2005, it marked the beginning of the modern hypercar era, combining extreme performance with near-luxury comfort highstuff.comhighstuff.com+1. The Veyron was conceived under Ferdinand Piëch’s vision to create the fastest, most technically ambitious production car, with development beginning after Volkswagen acquired Bugatti in 1998 mobilityground.commobilityground.com+1.
